Montana In a Nutshell

  • Montana population: 1.08 million as of september 2021
  • Montana was incorporated as a state: November 8th, 1889
  • Montana is located in the western region of the united states
  • Montana is in the northern rocky mountain region
  • Montana's average income: $60560

Montana, often referred to as "The Treasure State" or "Big Sky Country," is located in the western United States. It boasts stunning landscapes, including the Montana Rockies and famous national parks like Glacier and Yellowstone. The state's economy is diverse, with agriculture, mining, and tourism playing vital roles. Montana has a rich cultural heritage influenced by Native American traditions, and it is home to various Native American tribes. The state offers abundant outdoor activities, a rich wildlife heritage, and a strong cowboy culture. Its low population density and political diversity contribute to its unique character, making it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts, history buffs, and those interested in Native American culture alike.

Other information

Glacier National Park in Montana is a spectacular natural wonder, renowned for its diverse landscapes, glaciers, and abundant wildlife. Visitors can explore rugged mountains, pristine lakes, and dense forests while enjoying outdoor activities like hiking and wildlife watching. The iconic Going-to-the-Sun Road offers breathtaking views. This park, forming part of a UNESCO World Heritage Site, prioritizes conservation and is an international symbol of cooperation with Canada's Waterton Lakes National Park. Whether in summer or winter, Glacier National Park offers an unforgettable experience in the heart of the Rocky Mountains.
