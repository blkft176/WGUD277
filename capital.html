Helena, Montana, is the capital city of Montana, located in the Rocky Mountains. It has a population of around 32,000 people and a diverse economy with a focus on government, healthcare, education, and tourism. Helena has a rich history tied to the Montana Gold Rush and offers outdoor recreational opportunities, cultural attractions, and educational institutions. Its quality of life, blending urban amenities with outdoor access, makes it a desirable place to live. Helena is served by an airport and Interstate 15, providing transportation options.

Other information

Helena, Montana, has a rich Native American history. Before European settlement, various tribes, including the Salish, Shoshone, Blackfeet, Nez Perce, and Crow, inhabited the region. The Montana Gold Rush had a significant impact on indigenous communities, leading to conflicts and displacement. Treaties created reservations, such as the Blackfeet and Crow Reservations. Today, efforts are made to preserve and celebrate Native American cultures in the area, with spiritual and cultural significance still attached to the land. For a more detailed understanding, consult local sources and tribal representatives.
